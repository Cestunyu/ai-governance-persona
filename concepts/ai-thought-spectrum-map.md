# AI Thought Spectrum Map

Last updated: 2026-05-14

## Axes

Use two axes rather than a single doomer-to-optimist line.

### Risk / Governance Axis

| Score | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| -2 | Shutdown, pause, or hard doomer position |
| -1 | Safety-first, alignment-first, or strong governance |
| 0 | Normal-technology, social-harm, or hype-skeptical frame |
| +1 | Build-oriented, deployment-friendly optimism |
| +2 | Accelerationist or singularitarian optimism |

### Humanism / Anti-Anthropocentrism Axis

| Score | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| +2 | Democratic, justice-oriented, or explicitly human-centered |
| +1 | Human-compatible, human-survival, or human-preference centered |
| 0 | Technically neutral or mixed |
| -1 | Computation-centered or anti-hand-coded-human-knowledge |
| -2 | Anti-humanist, posthuman, or technocapital-autonomy frame |

## Reading Strategy

Start with seven primary texts that define the map's extreme and central positions:

1. `The Bitter Lesson` for computation-centered anti-anthropocentrism.
2. Yudkowsky's global-risk essay for rationalist AI doomerism.
3. Bostrom's superintelligence argument for instrumental convergence.
4. `Stochastic Parrots` for critical AI and present social harms.
5. Amodei's `Machines of Loving Grace` for frontier-lab safety optimism.
6. Andreessen's `Why AI Will Save the World` for techno-optimist anti-pause politics.
7. Land's `Meltdown` / `Dark Enlightenment` for the anti-humanist accelerationist edge.

## Influence Rubric

Use a 1-5 influence score:

- 5: field-defining or institution-shaping
- 4: major movement, policy, academic, or public-discourse influence
- 3: important niche, memetic, or genealogical influence
- 2: useful secondary influence
- 1: background only

Track the basis separately:

- academic citations
- lab or industry culture
- policy uptake
- institution-building
- public discourse
- movement formation
- capital/startup influence
- memetic influence

## Cautions

- `Doomer` and `AI safety` are not synonyms. Some AI safety work is technical and incremental; some is hard x-risk politics.
- `Optimistic` and `anti-humanist` are not synonyms. Andrew Ng and Nick Land are both anti-pause in different ways, but they do not belong in the same intellectual position.
- `Bitter Lesson` should not be over-politicized. Its relevance is that it weakens human-centered intuitions about how intelligence is built.
- `Critical AI` should not be treated as simply left-wing AI safety. It has a different object: social power, labor, race, data, environment, and institutions.
